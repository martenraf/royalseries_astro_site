We’ve all been there. You surrender your life to Jesus with a heart full of hope, only to find that “discipleship” feels like an endless to-do list. You’re told to pray more, serve more, and share your faith more. You’re carrying the heavy load of your responsibilities, your family, and your career—and now you’re carrying the “heavy load” of being a good Christian, too.

The truth? You were never meant to carry it. When we surrender to Jesus, the deal isn’t that He gives us a new set of chores; the deal is that He carries the load while we focus on staying close to Him.

The reason we feel drained is that we’ve been taught a definition of discipleship that leads with the Imperative (what we must do) while neglecting the Indicative (who we already are).  

## The 5 Ways We Try (and Fail) to Grow ##

Most of us have tried one of these five “models” of discipleship. They all have good parts, but they all have a “battery-drain” problem:  

### 1. The Information Model ###

**Strength:** You know your Bible.  
**Weakness:** You can know the Book without knowing the Author. It’s “data” without “drink.”

You study the Word for hours, accumulating knowledge, but knowledge alone doesn’t transform a weary soul. The information fills your head but leaves your heart empty.  

### 2. The Behavior Model ###

**Strength:** You look “put together.”   
**Weakness:** It’s pure performance. You’re a “Tired Pharisee” acting out a life you don’t actually feel.

You focus on moral discipline and external righteousness, but inside you’re collapsing under the weight of maintaining the image. Exhaustion becomes your constant companion.  

### 3. The Experience Model ###

**Strength:** Beautiful moments.  
**Weakness:** You’re a “Presence-Junkie.” When the music stops, you feel abandoned by God.

You chase the “high” of worship and emotional spiritual experiences, but these moments are fleeting. When they fade, so does your sense of God’s presence with you.

### 4. The Mission Model ###

**Strength:** Great impact.  
**Weakness:** You feel like a “cog in a machine.” If you aren’t producing results, you feel worthless.

You focus on “changing the world” and measuring spiritual maturity by output, but this leaves you burning out. Your worth becomes tied to your productivity rather than your identity.

### 5. The Relational Model ###

**Strength:** Human warmth.  
**Weakness:** The “Proxy” Problem. You rely on someone else’s faith. If the leader is dry, you stay dry because you haven’t learned to “drink” for yourself.

You rely on a mentor or a small group for spiritual nourishment, but what happens when that person isn’t available? You haven’t learned to access the Source directly.  

## The Royal Life Alternative: The Union-First Way ##

The Royal Life approach turns the table. We don’t start with what you can do; we start with your Union already given.

“A Disciple is someone who lives from Union, enjoys Communion, abides by Alignment, overflows in Participation, and changes in Sanctification.”
### How Royal Life Keeps the Strengths & Solves the Weaknesses: ###
   * It keeps the Word, but treats it as a “Software Update” that reminds you of your Father’s provision.
   * It keeps the Action, but makes it an Overflow of the River within you, not a “Work” to earn favor.
   * It keeps the Presence, but teaches that Felt Presence is experienced in Communion. Presence is not something to be worked up or generated; it’s something to be enjoyed through the permanent access you’ve already been given.
   * Because your Union with Christ is permanent, Communion becomes the disciple’s natural environment for Sanctification (inner change) and Participation (outer mission).
   * It transforms the Relational, moving you beyond “Life-on-Life” to “Life-from-the-Source.” You learn how your Union with Christ releases the River of Life personally. You learn to “drink” for yourself.

## The “Docking Station” Reality ##

Think of a disciple as a vacuum cleaning robot. Its mission is to clean the house, but its design requires it to return to the Docking Station. If it stays in the middle of the floor trying to “clean harder” on a dead battery, it’s useless.

Your “Docking Station” is your Union with Christ. When life feels heavy, it’s a signal that you’ve “undocked.” You’ve started believing the lie that it’s all on you.

Royal Life Discipleship is the art of Alignment. It’s the daily practice of renouncing the lie of self-reliance and inhabiting the truth that God has already provided everything you need.  

## Stop Pushing. Start Flowing. ## 

If you are tired of the “struggle,” it’s time to return to the Source. You don’t need more “to-dos.” You need a deeper realization of the Provision you already have.

The Christian life was never meant to be lived in the power of your own effort. It’s meant to be lived in the constant flow of the River of God—peace that transcends understanding, presence that sustains you, and power that transforms you from the inside out.

Ready to stop striving and start living from the River? My new book, “A Royal Life,” is a roadmap for believers who are tired of the performance trap. It will teach you how to dock, align, and let the River of Life flow through you to a world that needs it.
