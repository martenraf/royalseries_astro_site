## Life- instead of goal driven: How the River of Life flows through our Identity in Christ ##
For many of us in leadership, the Christian life has become a series of "Great Goals." We aim for the Great Commandment and the Great Commission. We strive for better worship, more mission, and deeper fellowship.
But when we treat these as drivers, we eventually run out of fuel. We find ourselves exhausted, trying to generate the power to meet the goal.
The shift we need is fundamental: The Christian life is not driven by goals; it is guided by them. It is driven by life—and that life has already been given to you in Christ.  

## The Source, The Posture, and The Expression ##
To live this "Royal Life," we must understand how our identity flows into our actions. If we confuse the categories, we lose the power. We don't need to generate momentum; we align with the momentum of Heaven that is already pressurized within us.
### 1. From Belonging to Worship ###
•	The Source (Identity): Justification, Adoption, and being the Temple of the Spirit.  
•	The Posture (Riverbanks): Communion with God.  
•	The Expression (Outlet): Worship.   
Because you are already right with God (Justified), a beloved child (Adoption), and His home (Temple), you don't worship to get His attention. You worship because you have His full attention. Worship is the outlet where you encounter His Glory. It is the overflow of a life in constant communion.
### 2. From Status to Family ###
•	The Source (Identity): Justification, Adoption, and the Temple.  
•	The Posture (Riverbanks): Communion with Brothers and Sisters.  
•	The Expression (Outlet): Fellowship.   
When you know your status is secure, you stop competing with others. You realize that the same Spirit in you is in them. Fellowship is the outlet where you experience His Love through the Body. It isn't just "hanging out"; it is the shared life of a Royal Family.
### 3. From Nature to Walk ###
•	The Source (Identity): Regeneration.  
•	The Posture (Riverbanks): Alignment.  
•	The Expression (Outlet): Obedience.   
This is a vital distinction: You don't obey to become holy. You were made a new creation (Regeneration), and now you align your walk with your new nature. Obedience is the outlet where you experience His Character manifest in your own life. It is simply the act of being who you truly are in Christ.
### 4. From Presence to Impact ###
•	The Source (Identity): Temple of the Spirit.  
•	The Posture (Riverbanks): Participation.  
•	The Expression (Outlet): Mission and Ministry.   
You don't go on mission for God; you participate with the Spirit who already dwells in you. Mission is the outlet where you encounter His Power. You are the vessel through which His life reaches the world.

## Why the Order Matters ##
When we reverse this logic, the Christian life falls apart. We try to use the "Outlets" to fill the "Source," which is impossible:  
•	Mission without being the Temple becomes activism and burnout.  
•	Obedience without Regeneration becomes legalism and "acting."  
•	Worship without Justification becomes a performance to please a distant Judge.  

### The Mind Shift: Inhabiting the Truth ###
If you feel "stuck," the answer is rarely "work harder." The answer is to return to the Source.
The "Royal Life" is maintained by faith. When we feel the "dryness" of effort, we repent—not just of our sins, but of our self-reliance. We renounce the lie that we are lacking, and we inhabit the truth of God’s provision.
Say to Him: *"Thank You, Father, for Your provision. I believe that because I am Your temple and Your child, I have the life I need for today. I renounce the lie that I must generate my own strength, and I choose to participate in Your flow."*
### Final Word ###
The Christian life is not a machine to be fueled by your effort; it is a River to be inhabited by your faith. Our posture (communion, alignment etc.) forms the riverbanks that guide you, but the Holy Spirit is the water that carries you. The goals (worship, fellowship, obedience etc.) are given by God to experience His life. 
These goals are not burdens; they are the beautiful outlets where you get to witness the momentum of Heaven breaking into earth. You don't produce the momentum—you align with it.
Stop trying to produce the river. Just let it flow.
