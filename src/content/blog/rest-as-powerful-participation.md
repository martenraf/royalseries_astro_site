Many Christians are tired.

Not because they do too little. But because they do it in the wrong order.

They love Jesus. They long for growth. They want to make a difference.

And yet their faith sometimes feels like a sprint on a treadmill.

Pray harder. Do your best. Even more discipline.

And somewhere, deep inside, the question gnaws: Is it ever enough?  
  
## The Trap of Trying ##

We have subconsciously come to believe that rest is a reward. Something you get at the end of the ride, if you have done your best.

We try to work towards God. In the hope that He is satisfied. In the hope that we will finally feel that deep peace.

But what if rest is not the end station? What if it’s the fuel?  

## Yoke Invitation ##

Jesus says:

*“Come to me, all you who are weary and heavy laden, and I will give you rest.” —Matthew 11:28*  

Look carefully at what He says there.

He doesn’t invite you for a vacation of a lifetime. It does not invite you to sit passively on the couch.

He proposes an exchange.

Your heavy yoke — the yoke of “must” and “prove” — for His. For His yoke is easy. His burden is light.

A yoke is meant to move. To work.

The rest that Jesus promises is not a standstill. It’s participation.  

## The Order of the King’s Son ##

In a royal life, your position is already fixed.

You don’t have to work to become a child of God. You already are.

That is Union: the indestructible connection with Christ. Not deserved, but received.

When you take that peace of your identity as a starting point, everything changes:  
    1. Doing becomes moving along  
    2. Toil becomes boarding  
    3. Fear becomes trust  

You no longer work towards acceptance. You live from acceptance.  

## The Monday Morning Shift ##

What does that look like when the alarm goes off?

**Old reflex:** *“Lord, help me to be a good Christian today. Forgive me for my mistakes yesterday and help me do better today.”*

That’s the treadmill. It is focused on your performance.

**New movement:** *“Thank You, Father, that I am Your beloved child today. Thank You for being in this day already. I step into Your rest and I do what I do from Your strength.”*

Do you see the difference?

You don’t try harder. You trust more deeply.

You don’t generate your own peace. You inhabit the peace that is already there.  

## The Step into the Truth ##

The truth of the Gospel is not a theory that you have to understand. It is a reality in which you can stand.

*Inhabiting the truth.*

You don’t just say, “I am justified.” You start to move like someone who is no longer guilty.

You don’t serve to be seen. You serve because you have already been seen.

That is the freedom of the King’s Son.

You don’t have to stop with your Christian life. But you can stop toiling in your own strength.

Don’t stop. But get in.
